# Business Analysis Framework

## Objective

Determine whether a company is fundamentally a high-quality business capable of creating long-term shareholder value.

---

# Analysis Areas

## 1. Business Model

Questions

- What does the company do?
- How does it make money?
- Is the business easy to understand?
- Is the revenue model predictable?
- Is demand sustainable?

Output

Business Model Rating:
Excellent / Good / Average / Weak

---

## 2. Industry

Questions

- Which industry does the company operate in?
- Is the industry growing?
- Is the industry cyclical?
- What are the long-term trends?

Red Flags

- Declining industry
- Regulatory disruption
- Technological obsolescence

---

## 3. Competitive Advantage (Moat)

Questions

- Does the company have pricing power?
- Brand strength?
- Cost advantage?
- Network effects?
- High switching costs?
- Patents or intellectual property?

Output

Moat Rating:
Strong / Moderate / Weak / None

---

## 4. Customers

Questions

- Who are the customers?
- Is customer concentration high?
- Is customer demand recurring?
- Are customers sticky?

Red Flags

- One customer contributes >30% revenue
- Declining customer base

---

## 5. Revenue Quality

Questions

- Is revenue recurring?
- Is growth organic?
- Is growth consistent?
- Is growth sustainable?

Red Flags

- Sudden unexplained revenue spikes
- Heavy acquisition-driven growth

---

## 6. Scalability

Questions

- Can revenue grow faster than costs?
- Can the business expand internationally?
- Is additional capital required for growth?

Output

Scalability Rating:
High / Medium / Low

---

## 7. Risks

Evaluate

- Regulatory Risk
- Technology Risk
- Customer Risk
- Supplier Risk
- Competition Risk
- Currency Risk
- Political Risk
